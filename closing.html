🤝 Module 8

Closing Clients

Closing is not pressure — it's guidance. A prospect who loved the demo just needs a clear, confident path to say yes. This module gives you every tool: reading buying signals, presenting pricing, handling hesitation, and asking for commitment naturally.

📡 Recognising Buying Signals
Buying signals are moments when the prospect has already decided internally — they just haven't said yes out loud yet. Miss these and you keep presenting. Catch them and close immediately.
🟢 Strong buying signals — close immediately
💰
"How much does it cost?"
The strongest buying signal. They're not asking to find a reason to say no — they're asking because they want it and need to know if they can afford it.
→ "Great question — let me walk you through that now. The build is completely done, so what we put in place is a monthly hosting and maintenance arrangement of ₦[X]. That keeps the site live, updated, and supported. Want me to get that set up for you?"
🔧
"Can you add our logo / change this colour / add more cars?"
They're mentally customising it — which means they're imagining themselves owning it. This is ownership language.
→ "Absolutely — that's all part of the setup. I'll make every change you want before we go live. What else would you like adjusted?"
📅
"When can it be live?"
They're already thinking about the future with the website. This is a green light — they just want a timeline.
→ "Once we confirm today, I can have it fully live with your domain within 48 hours. Shall we move forward?"
👥
"I want to show this to my team / partner"
They like it enough to involve others — which means they've personally decided yes. They want internal validation, not a reason to say no.
→ "Of course — the link is on your WhatsApp, share it with them. Would it help if I joined a quick 10-minute call with you both so I can answer any questions directly?"
😮
"Wow, this is exactly what we needed" / "This is really good"
Emotional validation. They've connected emotionally — now is the moment to ask for commitment before the emotion fades.
→ "I'm really glad. So shall we get this running for [Dealership Name]? I can start the setup today."
🤔
"What happens if I want to cancel?"
They're thinking about commitment and managing risk — which means they're considering saying yes. This is not resistance; it's due diligence.
→ "It's completely month-to-month — no contract, no lock-in. If for any reason it's not working for you, you simply let me know and that's it. Zero risk. Shall we get started?"
⚡ The buying signal rule
The moment you hear a buying signal — stop presenting and start closing. Most salespeople keep pitching after the buyer is already convinced, which creates doubt where there was none. When they signal yes, move to the ask immediately.
🎯 Closing Techniques
Six natural closes that don't feel like selling. Click each to expand the full script and when to use it.
1
The Assumptive Close
Assume they're moving forward — let them stop you if not
⌄
Script "Great — so let me get the setup started. I'll need your preferred domain name and the WhatsApp number you want customers to reach you on. What would you like the domain to be — something like [dealershipname].com.ng?"
When to use: After a clear positive reaction or a buying signal. You're not asking if they want to proceed — you're beginning the setup process. If they're not ready, they'll say so. Most of the time, they go along.
2
The Summary Close
Recap value, then ask — one final yes to everything
⌄
Script "So just to summarise what you're getting — a fully built website with your dealership's name, a searchable inventory your customers can browse on their phones, an enquiry form that sends structured leads directly to you, and ongoing hosting and maintenance to keep everything running. All for ₦[X] per month. Shall we get it live?"
When to use: When they seem interested but slightly uncertain. The recap reminds them of everything they're getting before the ask — makes the price feel smaller relative to the value.
3
The Question Close
Ask what's stopping them — then solve it
⌄
Script "Based on what you've seen — is there anything stopping you from getting this running for your dealership today?"
When to use: When the mood is positive but they haven't said yes. If they give a reason, you now know exactly what to address. If they say "nothing" — close immediately.
4
The Urgency Close
Create honest, real urgency — not fake pressure
⌄
Script "I'm currently building websites for a few dealerships in [City] at the same time. The one that goes live first has the advantage — they'll be indexed on Google before anyone else in their area. If you want to be ahead of your competition, now is the time. Shall I get yours live first?"
When to use: For competitive dealership owners who care about being first. Never create fake urgency — only use this when it's genuinely true.
5
The ROI Close
Connect the monthly cost to one car sale
⌄
Script "Let me put the cost in context. Your hosting is ₦[X] per month. If the website brings you even one extra car sale per month that you wouldn't have gotten through Instagram or WhatsApp alone — what's your profit on a typical sale? Even a small saloon car is ₦[Y] profit. The website pays for itself 10× over from a single deal. Does that make the numbers work for you?"
When to use: When they're hesitating on the monthly price. Connecting cost to a single sale makes the ROI immediately obvious to a business owner.
6
The Take-Away Close
Genuine willingness to walk away creates urgency naturally
⌄
Script "Look — I don't want to push this if the timing isn't right for you. I genuinely think this would help your dealership, but the decision is completely yours. If it's not the right moment, no hard feelings — I'll reach out again in a few months. What would you like to do?"
When to use: When they've been stalling for too long and nothing else is working. Genuine willingness to leave paradoxically makes people want to commit. Use sparingly.
💰 Presenting Pricing
How you introduce the price matters as much as the number itself. Follow this exact sequence.
1
Anchor with the free build
"The website itself — everything you've seen — is completely built and ready. There's no charge for the build. That's done."
2
Introduce what they're paying for
"What we put in place is a simple monthly arrangement that covers hosting — keeping the website live on your domain — plus ongoing maintenance so the site stays updated as your inventory changes, and technical support whenever you need it."
3
State the price simply
"That's ₦[X] per month." — Then stop. Don't justify it immediately. Let the number land.
4
Add the ROI framing
"To put that in perspective — one extra customer per month from the website pays for it many times over. And the website works for you 24 hours a day, seven days a week, even when your team isn't online."
5
Remove the risk
"It's month-to-month — no contract, no lock-in. If you're ever not happy, you simply let me know. There's no risk to getting started."
Pricing rules
✗ Never mention price before the demo — value must come first
✗ Never apologise for the price or say "it's only ₦X" — it signals that you think it's expensive
✗ Never offer a discount before they've even objected — you're giving money away unnecessarily
✓ State the price confidently, pause, and let them respond before speaking again
✓ Always frame price against the cost of a single car sale — makes the number feel minimal
✓ Month-to-month = no risk. Say this every time you present pricing
🤔 Handling Hesitation at the Close
Post-demo hesitation is different from cold-call objections. They've seen the value — they just need a little more confidence to commit.
"I need to think about it."
"Of course — what specifically would you need to think through? I want to make sure you have everything you need to make a confident decision. Is it the cost, or something about the website itself?"
Surfaces the real objection. "Think about it" is almost always a specific unresolved concern — uncover it and address it directly.
"The price is a bit high."
"I understand. Let me ask — what's a typical profit on one car sale for you? [They answer] So the website pays for itself entirely from one deal per month that you might not have gotten without it. Does that change how you see the number?"
Never discount before using the ROI frame first. Make them do the maths — it's more convincing when they calculate it themselves.
"Let me discuss with my wife / partner / business partner."
"That makes complete sense — this is a business decision and it's smart to involve them. Would it be helpful if I sent a short summary of what's included on WhatsApp so you have something concrete to share? And if they have any questions, I'm happy to jump on a quick call with you both."
Send the summary immediately so the conversation happens with facts, not vague memories. Offering to join the partner call dramatically increases close rate.
"Can you reduce the price?"
"I appreciate you asking directly. The price is set to cover the full hosting, maintenance, and support — cutting it would mean cutting the service. What I can do is make sure you get the maximum value from day one: I'll update the inventory personally for the first month, set up your domain, and be on standby for any changes. Is that fair?"
Don't discount — add value instead. Offering extra hands-on support in the first month maintains your price and increases their confidence.
"I'm not sure this will bring customers."
"That's a fair concern. I can't guarantee a specific number — no one honestly can. What I can tell you is that right now your dealership doesn't exist to anyone searching Google for a car in [City]. A website changes that immediately. Would you be comfortable giving it one month to see what happens? The risk is minimal — it's month-to-month."
Month-to-month removes the risk of a long commitment. One month to test is a very easy yes for most business owners.
"We'll start next month."
"Of course — I can lock in everything now and go live on the date you choose. That way the setup is done and you don't have to chase it later. Shall I prepare it now and we activate on [specific date next month]?"
Accept the timing, but secure the commitment now. Doing the setup immediately keeps momentum and prevents the deal from going cold.
💳 Transitioning to Payment
Once they say yes, move to payment naturally and quickly. Don't give the deal time to go cold.
The payment transition script
Once they say yes "Perfect — let's get it sorted right now while we're on the call. I'll send you the payment details on WhatsApp. It's ₦[X] for the first month — after that it renews monthly and I'll remind you before each renewal. Does that work?"
After payment confirmation "Got it — thank you. I'll start the domain setup now and send you a confirmation once everything is live. I'll have it ready within 48 hours. You're going to love having this running. Welcome to StoreLift! 🎉"
✓ Payment best practices
→ Send payment details on WhatsApp within 2 minutes of them saying yes
→ Accept bank transfer or mobile money — whichever is easiest for them
→ Confirm receipt immediately and start work the same day
→ Send a simple receipt or invoice on WhatsApp for their records
→ Set a reminder to follow up 48 hours later with the live website link
✗ Payment mistakes to avoid
→ Don't wait until after the call to send payment details — do it live
→ Don't accept "I'll pay later" without a specific date confirmed
→ Don't start work before payment — even friendly clients can go silent
→ Don't disappear after payment — communicate every step of the setup
→ Don't over-promise the go-live date — under-promise, over-deliver
