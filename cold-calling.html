The Opening — First 20 Seconds
This is the most practised part of your call. It determines everything.
😁 Smile before dialling 🐢 Speak slower than feels natural ⚡ High energy opener ⏸ Pause after their name
SMILE "Hello, good day — PAUSE please is this [Name]?" — Wait for confirmation — ENERGY UP "Alright! My name is Elijah — I run a web design agency called StoreLift. SLOW DOWN I came across your dealership on Instagram this morning and I noticed you have some really nice units — I saw the 2020 Highlander you posted." PAUSE 1 SEC "But I noticed something — I went through your page and I couldn't find a full list of your current inventory anywhere. Someone in the comments was asking about pricing and hadn't gotten a reply. SLOW I think you might be losing customers right there who won't bother to DM."
⚡ Why this opening works
You lead with a specific observation about their business — not a generic pitch. You name something real you saw on their page. This proves you're not randomly dialling and immediately separates you from every other cold caller they've ever received. The "I think you're losing customers" line creates a moment of curiosity and mild concern that makes them want to hear more.
🔀
Opening Variations
Different situations, different openers. All use the same structure.
Variation A — No Instagram, found via referral or Google
"Hello, good day — is this [Name]? My name is Elijah from StoreLift. I came across [Dealership Name] while I was researching car dealerships in [City] and I noticed you don't seem to have a website where customers can browse your full inventory. I actually built something I'd love to show you."
Variation B — They have a website but it's outdated/poor
"Hello, good day — is this [Name]? My name is Elijah from StoreLift. I visited your website this morning and I noticed it hasn't been updated in a while — your inventory isn't showing there and there's no way for customers to enquire on specific cars. I rebuilt it completely for you — I'd love to show you what I came up with."
Variation C — TikTok dealership with viral content
"Hello, good day — is this [Name]? My name is Elijah from StoreLift. I saw one of your TikTok videos — the walkthrough on the Camry got a lot of traction. But I noticed everyone in the comments asking 'where are you located' and 'do you ship' — and there was nowhere to send them. I built you a proper home base for all that traffic."
🚪
Speaking to the Gatekeeper
Receptionist or front desk. Be warm, brief, and always aim to be routed through.
When the gatekeeper picks up:
"Hello, good afternoon. Please is [Owner Name / the owner / the manager] available? My name is Elijah — I'm a web developer and I have something I'd like to show them about the dealership's online presence."
If they ask: "What is it about?"
"I came across the dealership on Instagram and I built a website for them — I just want to show it to the owner quickly. It won't take more than 5 minutes of their time."
If they say the owner isn't available:
"No problem at all. Is there a direct number or WhatsApp I can reach them on? I just don't want to keep calling the main line — I'd rather contact them directly so I'm not wasting anyone's time."
If they won't give a direct number:
"Understood. Could you let them know Elijah from StoreLift called about their website? I'll send a WhatsApp to this number and they can reach back when it's convenient. Thank you so much."
✓ Golden rules for gatekeepers
1. Never over-explain to the gatekeeper — they can't say yes and over-explaining gives them reasons to say no on the owner's behalf.
2. Be warm and respectful — Nigerian gatekeepers respond to courtesy. "Please" and "Thank you so much" go a long way.
3. Always ask for a WhatsApp or direct number before you leave the call.
4. If they promise to pass a message — follow up with a WhatsApp to the main number within 10 minutes while they still remember you.
👔
Speaking to a Sales Representative
They can't make decisions but they can route you to someone who can — or block you entirely.
When you realise it's a sales rep, not the owner:
"Oh okay — are you part of the sales team? That's great. Who handles decisions about things like the dealership's website and online presence — is that the owner directly?"
If they ask what it's about before connecting you:
"I'm a web developer — I built a website for the dealership that I want to show the owner. It's something that would help the sales team too actually, because customers could browse cars and send in enquiries directly without having to message on WhatsApp first. Less back-and-forth for everyone."
If they say "the owner is busy, just tell me":
"I totally understand — and I don't want to waste anyone's time. It's a quick visual thing, so it really needs to go to whoever makes decisions. Could you let them know Elijah from StoreLift called? And please ask them what time is best for me to call back — I'll respect their schedule."
💡 Make the sales rep an ally
The smart move is to briefly explain how a website benefits them personally — less WhatsApp answering, more qualified leads, easier job. A sales rep who sees personal value will actively help you reach the owner instead of blocking you.
👑
Speaking Directly to the Owner
This is the conversation that matters. Respect their time, earn their curiosity.
SMILE "Hello, good day — please is this [Owner Name]?" — Confirmed — ENERGY "Brilliant! My name is Elijah — I run a web design agency called StoreLift. I came across [Dealership Name] on Instagram this morning." PAUSE SLOW DOWN "I was looking through your page and I have good news and some bad news. The good news is — your inventory looks great, you have some really nice units. The bad news is — I went through your whole page and I couldn't find a way to actually browse everything you have in one place. I had to scroll through weeks of posts just to find the Highlander, and I still couldn't get a price without messaging you directly." PAUSE — let that land "A lot of your potential customers won't do all that. They'll just go somewhere else." PAUSE "So here's why I'm calling — ENERGY I actually built a website for your dealership already. All your cars listed, searchable by make and model, with prices and photos. Anyone interested clicks a button and their details come straight to you — no back and forth on WhatsApp." SLOW "I'm not asking you to commit to anything. I just want 15 minutes to show you what I put together. If you like it, great — we talk. If not, no problem at all, you go your way." PAUSE "Is your mornings or afternoons generally better for a quick call?"
📅
Booking the Demo Call
Close for the appointment — not the sale. This is your only goal.
The appointment close:
"Give me 3 days to put the finishing touches on it and I'll reach out to schedule. Are mornings or afternoons generally better for you?"
Once they pick a time — lock it immediately:
"Perfect — so I'll send you a WhatsApp on [day] and we'll confirm a time. What's the best number to reach you on WhatsApp?"
Send the WhatsApp within 2 minutes of hanging up:
"Hi [Name] 👋 This is Elijah from StoreLift — we just spoke about your dealership website. I'm putting the final touches on it now and will reach out on [day] to show you. Looking forward to it!"
📌 Booking rules — never break these
1. Always give binary choice: "mornings or afternoons?" — never "when are you free?"
2. Always get their WhatsApp number before ending the call — even if they already called you on it.
3. Send the WhatsApp within 2 minutes — while they still remember you.
4. Never book for the same day — give yourself at least 1 day so the website is ready and the demo looks polished.
5. Set a reminder to follow up the morning of the demo to confirm they're still available.
🔁
Follow-Up Sequence
Most deals are closed on follow-up, not the first call. Here's the exact sequence.
Day 0
📞 First call — book the demo
Send WhatsApp immediately after hanging up. "Hi [Name], this is Elijah from StoreLift — we just spoke. I'll reach out on [day] to show you the website. Looking forward to it! 👋"
Day 3
💬 Demo day WhatsApp — morning confirmation
"Good morning [Name] 👋 This is Elijah. The website for [Dealership] is ready — are you still good for a quick 15-minute call today? Just let me know what time works and I'll send you the link."
Day 5
📞 No response — second call
"Hello [Name], this is Elijah from StoreLift — I reached out on WhatsApp a couple of days ago about the website I built for your dealership. I just wanted to make sure you got the message. I'm still happy to show you — just 15 minutes. When would work for you?"
Day 8
💬 Final WhatsApp — soft close
"Hi [Name], Elijah here from StoreLift. I've followed up a couple of times — I don't want to keep disturbing you. The website for [Dealership] is ready whenever you are. If it's not a priority right now, completely fine — I'll check back in a few weeks. Just say the word. 🙏"
Day 21
📞 Re-engagement — new angle
"Hello [Name], Elijah from StoreLift. I know it's been a few weeks — I just wanted to check in. I noticed [specific new thing about their page — new posts, a sale, something real]. How's business going? I still have your website ready any time you want to see it."

Voice & Tone Guide
Where to slow down, where to pause, where to smile, and where to bring energy.
Vocal control by call phase
Speed Opener: SLOW · Pain point: VERY SLOW · Offer: MEDIUM · Close: DELIBERATE
Energy Opener: HIGH · Pain: CALM · Offer: EXCITED (controlled) · Close: CONFIDENT
Warmth Throughout: HIGH — this is relationship-first selling
🐢 Where to slow down
• When you say your name and company
• When you name the specific pain point
• When you say "I built something for your dealership"
• When you ask for the appointment
• Any time you feel yourself rushing
⏸ Where to pause
• After asking "Is this [Name]?" — wait for them
• After naming the pain — let it land
• After saying "I built something for you" — build suspense
• After presenting the offer — don't fill the silence
• After asking for the appointment — wait for their answer
😁 Where to smile
• Before the call connects — already smiling
• When they confirm they are the owner
• When you say "I built something for you"
• When they ask "how much?" — that's a buying signal
• When you close for the appointment
⚡ Where to bring energy
• The very first sentence — grab their attention
• When you describe what the website does for them
• When you say "completely free to build"
• When you book the appointment — sound excited
• Your sign-off — leave them feeling positive

What Never to Say
These phrases kill calls instantly. Memorise them so you never say them by accident.
❌ Never say this ✓ Say this instead Why it matters
"I just wanted to quickly..." "My name is Elijah — I came across your dealership..." "Just" and "quickly" signal you don't believe your own call is worth their time. Start with confidence.
"Sorry to bother you..." "Good afternoon, is this [Name]?" Apologising before you've said anything positions you as an inconvenience. You're offering something valuable.
"When are you free?" "Are mornings or afternoons better for you?" Open questions about availability put the burden on them and often result in "I'll let you know" — which means never.
"I promise it will be worth it..." "If you like it, great. If not, no problem at all." Promising value sounds desperate. Detachment sounds confident. Confident people get meetings.
"We are the best in the market..." "I built something specifically for your dealership — let me show you." Generic claims about being "the best" are meaningless. Specificity to their business is what gets attention.
"Do you have a website?" "I noticed you don't have a website where customers can browse your full inventory..." Asking a question they answer with yes or no hands them control. Make an observation instead — it's more compelling.
"It's completely free, no catch..." "The website itself is free — the build is on me." "No catch" makes people think there IS a catch. State what's free simply and move on.
"Please sir/ma, just give me a chance..." "I understand — no problem at all. Can I reach out in a few weeks?" Begging destroys all credibility instantly. Walk away gracefully — it's more likely to bring them back than begging is.
"I can also do social media and..." Stay focused on the website only Adding services mid-call confuses the offer. One thing, one demo, one decision. Extra services come after they're a client.
"The hosting is ₦X per month..." Defer all pricing to the demo call Bringing up money on the first call before they've seen the website gives them something to object to before they're invested.
