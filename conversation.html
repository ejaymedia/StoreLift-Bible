Conversation Flow

🗺️ The 7-Stage Conversation Map
Click any stage to expand the full breakdown — what to say, what to watch for, and how to transition naturally to the next stage.
👋
Stage 1 — Greeting
0–15 sec
Confirm identity, state your name, set a warm and confident tone before anything else.
The greeting "Hello, good day — please is this [Name]?"

[Wait for confirmation]

"Alright! My name is Elijah — I run a web design agency called StoreLift."
✓ Do
→ Smile before the call connects
→ Speak slowly and clearly
→ Sound like you expected them to pick up
→ Pause after confirming their name
✗ Don't
→ Start with "sorry to bother you"
→ Rush through your name
→ Say "I just wanted to quickly..."
→ Sound surprised they answered
Transition: Once they confirm who they are, move immediately into the observation about their business. No filler.
🤝
Stage 2 — Rapport
15–45 sec
Show you did your homework. Compliment something specific and real about their business.
Building rapport "I came across your dealership on Instagram this morning — you have some really nice units. I saw the 2020 Highlander you posted and the Lexus ES you had last week. Your page is active, I can see you're putting in the work."
💡 The rapport rule
Always mention something specific — a car model, a recent post, something you genuinely saw. Generic compliments ("your page looks nice") are forgettable and unconvincing. Specific observations prove you looked and make them lower their guard.
✓ Specific (works)
→ "I saw the 2020 Camry you posted Tuesday"
→ "Your TikTok on the Highlander got good engagement"
→ "I noticed someone in the comments asking for a price"
✗ Generic (fails)
→ "Your page looks great"
→ "You seem to be doing well"
→ "I like your business"
😤
Stage 3 — Identifying Pain
45 sec – 1:30
Name the gap you observed. Make them feel the problem before you offer the solution.
Naming the pain "But I noticed something — I went through your whole page and there's no way to actually see all your current inventory in one place. Someone in the comments was asking about pricing and it hadn't been answered. A lot of your potential customers won't bother to DM — they'll just move on."
Pain questions — use these to deepen the pain naturally:
"How many WhatsApp messages do you get daily asking what cars you have available?"
"When someone finds your Instagram at 11pm and wants to browse your inventory — what happens?"
"Do you feel like your Instagram ads are converting well, or are most people just enquiring without buying?"
⚠ The pain delivery rule
Say the pain statement and then pause. Don't rush to the solution. Let it land. Silence after naming a real problem is powerful — it gives them a moment to agree with you internally. Filling the silence immediately weakens the impact.
🤔
Stage 4 — Creating Curiosity
1:30 – 2:00
Tease the solution without revealing everything. Make them want to see it.
The curiosity hook "So here's why I'm calling — I actually built a website for your dealership already. All your cars listed and searchable, with pricing, photos, and a simple enquiry form. Customers browse on their own and send you their details directly — no WhatsApp back-and-forth needed."
What makes curiosity work:
Specificity
Say "I built a website for YOUR dealership" — not "I can build a website." Already done = more powerful.
The open loop
Don't describe every feature. Give just enough that they want to see it. Incomplete information creates curiosity.
Outcomes not features
Don't say "it has a homepage, about page, and inventory page." Say "customers browse and enquire directly — less WhatsApp chaos."
No cost mention yet
Pricing kills curiosity before value is established. Don't mention money until the demo.
💡
Stage 5 — Presenting the Solution
2:00 – 2:30
State the offer simply. Free website, 15 minutes to show it. No pressure.
Presenting the offer "I'm not asking you to commit to anything right now. The website build itself is completely free — I just want 15 minutes to show you what I put together for your dealership. If you like it, we'll talk about what comes next. If not, no problem at all — we go our separate ways."
🧠 Why this framing works
"No commitment" removes risk. "If you like it, great. If not, no problem" shows detachment — which signals confidence. The Nigerian business owner is now thinking: "what do I have to lose?" The answer is nothing. That's when they say yes.
✓ Say this
→ "Just 15 minutes of your time"
→ "Completely free to build"
→ "If you like it, great — if not, no problem"
→ "No commitment needed"
✗ Never say
→ "It won't cost you anything, I promise"
→ "Please just give me a chance"
→ "The hosting is only ₦X per month"
→ "I think you'll really love it"
📅
Stage 6 — Booking the Demo
2:30 – 3:00
Close for the appointment using a binary choice. Lock it down before hanging up.
The booking close "Give me 3 days to finish it up and I'll reach out to schedule the call. Are mornings or afternoons generally better for you?"

[They answer]

"Perfect. And the best WhatsApp to reach you on is this number?"

[Confirm number]

"Brilliant — I'll send you a message right now so you have my contact, and I'll reach out on [day] to set the time. Looking forward to showing you what I built."
✓ Green signal
"How much does it cost?"
They're already thinking about paying. Defer to the demo: "That's exactly what I'll walk you through on the call."
✓ Green signal
"What does it look like?"
Curiosity confirmed. "That's what I want to show you — it's much better seen than described."
⚡ Warm signal
"Okay, send me something first"
They want proof before committing. Send the WhatsApp link and follow up. Don't abandon — follow up twice.
⚠ Stall signal
"Call me tomorrow"
Polite deflection. Try to lock in a specific time now: "Of course — is morning or afternoon better tomorrow?" Don't leave it open.
🎯
Stage 7 — Ending Professionally
Final 15 sec
Leave them feeling respected and positive. Your last impression is what they remember.
Professional sign-off "Great — I'll let you get back to it. I'm sending the WhatsApp now so you have my number. Talk to you on [day], have a great day!"
If they said no — end gracefully:
"Completely understood — I appreciate your time. Would it be alright if I check back in a few weeks? Business needs change and I want to make sure you have the option when the timing is right."
✓ The 2-minute WhatsApp rule
Within 2 minutes of hanging up — whether they said yes or no — send a brief WhatsApp. Yes: confirm the demo. No: leave a positive impression that keeps the door open. Nigerian business owners talk to each other. A gracious follow-up on a no can still generate a referral.

"And that's the problem I built something to solve..." "So the question is..." "I just want 15 minutes to show you..." "If you like it, great — if not, no pressure..."
