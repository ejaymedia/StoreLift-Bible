📋 The Daily StoreLift Workflow
This is your operating system. Run this every weekday without exception. Consistency is what separates one-off lucky closes from a real pipeline.
🌅
Morning Prep — Read & Research
6:00 – 7:00am
Start your day before you pick up the phone. Read your daily motivation from the Dashboard. Identify 10–15 dealerships to call today by searching Instagram hashtags and location tags. For each one, note one specific thing — a car model, a recent post, a comment that went unanswered.
💡 Quality research = confident calls. You sound 3× more credible when you mention something specific.
📞
First Calling Block
7:00 – 10:00am
This is your prime calling window. Nigerian business owners are most reachable between 7am and 10am — before the day gets chaotic. Make your first 15–20 calls here. Stand up, smile, and work through your list systematically. After every 5 calls, take a 3-minute break to reset your energy.
💡 The first call of the day is the hardest. Do it immediately — don't warm up with admin. Build momentum by dialling first.
🖥️
Website Building / Demo Prep
10:00am – 12:00pm
Use this block for building websites for any dealerships you booked demos with. Also prep any demos happening today — test links, confirm inventory is populated, rehearse your walkthrough. One well-prepared demo converts at 2× the rate of an unprepared one.
💡 You have 3 days to build each website. Start the same day you book the demo — not the night before.
🍽️
Lunch & Rest
12:00 – 1:00pm
Step away from the work completely. Eat, rest, move your body. Sales is mentally demanding — a proper break means your afternoon energy is higher than if you pushed through.
💡 Fatigue is the enemy of a good cold call. Your voice, energy, and patience all drop when you're tired.
📞
Second Calling Block + Follow-Ups
1:00 – 4:00pm
Afternoon calling for the remaining leads on your list. Also use this block for follow-up calls to anyone you called this morning and couldn't reach, and WhatsApp follow-ups to prospects who haven't responded from previous days. Afternoon demos can also happen in this window.
💡 1–4pm is when business owners slow down and are sometimes more willing to have a brief conversation than during the morning rush.
🖥️
Demo Window
4:00 – 6:00pm
Keep this window reserved for demo calls. Business owners are often more settled in the late afternoon — decisions about staff, inventory, and operations have been made, and they can give you focused attention. Send your 30-minute reminder at 3:30pm.
💡 If a demo gets cancelled, use the slot for prospecting or WhatsApp follow-ups. Never let the time go to waste.
📝
End-of-Day Review
6:00 – 6:30pm
Log your numbers in the Results Tracker. Note the calls that went well and the ones that didn't — one specific lesson from each. Update your pipeline: who needs a follow-up tomorrow? Who booked a demo? Who closed? Prepare your list for tomorrow morning.
💡 The 30 minutes you spend reviewing today multiplies the quality of tomorrow. Never skip the review.
🔍 Finding Nigerian Dealership Leads
Where to find dealerships, how to qualify them quickly, and what information to gather before calling.
Finding on Instagram
📸 Hashtag Search
Search: #carsales, #lagoscars, #abujacars, #nigeriacars, #usedcarsnigeria, #tokunbocars
Filter by Recent — not Top — so you find active accounts
Look for accounts with 500–10,000 followers posting inventory regularly
Check if they have a website link in bio — no link = perfect prospect
📍 Location Tag Search
Search Lagos, Abuja, Port Harcourt, Ibadan location tags
Browse car-related posts from those locations
Note dealerships posting frequently — active = serious business
🔗 Explore & Similar Accounts
When you find one dealership, check who they follow and who follows them
Instagram suggests similar accounts — work through those
One good account can lead to 10 similar prospects
Other Lead Sources
🛒 Jiji & Cars45
Browse Jiji.ng car listings — dealers post frequently
Look for dealers with multiple listings (5+ = serious dealership)
Their contact number is usually right there — call directly
Look for their Instagram page name in the listing description
🗺️ Google Maps
Search "car dealership [city name]" in Google Maps
Many will show a phone number and website (or lack of one)
No website = immediate prospect
Check their reviews for clues about their reputation and activity
📘 Facebook Groups
Join groups: "Cars for Sale Lagos", "Abuja Car Market", "Nigeria Car Dealers"
Find active dealers posting inventory — message for their contact number
Look for dealers with consistent posts but no website link
✅ Quick Qualification — Is This Worth Calling?
✓ Good prospect (call them)
→ Active on Instagram (posts in last 7 days)
→ Selling real inventory (cars clearly shown)
→ No website link in bio
→ Getting engagement (comments, DMs visible)
→ Contact number visible or findable
→ Appears to be actual dealership (multiple car types)
✗ Skip for now
→ Last post was 3+ weeks ago (inactive)
→ Already has a good, working website
→ Only 1–2 cars listed (too small)
→ No contact number anywhere
→ Appears to be individual reseller, not dealership
→ Already declined previously (give 4 weeks before retry)
📋 What to note before calling each dealership
✓ Dealership name
✓ Instagram handle
✓ Phone number
✓ City / area
✓ One specific recent post to mention
✓ Car model to reference on the call
✓ Any unanswered comments to cite
✓ Whether they run ads (check for Sponsored posts)

📆 Weekly Review Framework
Every Sunday, spend 30 minutes reviewing the week. This is where your skills compound — you get better every single week if you do this consistently.
📊
Numbers Review
Look at your weekly tracker data
Did I make at least 150 calls this week (30/day × 5 days)?
What was my reach rate — what percentage of calls got through to a real person?
Of conversations had, what percentage became booked demos?
Of demos held, what percentage closed?
Where is the biggest drop-off in my funnel this week?
🎙️
Call Quality Review
Listen to 3 recorded calls from this week
Did I sound confident and unhurried on my opener?
Did I make specific observations about each dealership, or was I generic?
Did I pause after stating the pain point — or rush to the solution?
Did I use binary close ("mornings or afternoons") or open question ("when are you free?")?
What's one phrase I said that I should replace with something better?
🎯
Demo Quality Review
Review any demos held this week
Did I open the demo by setting the customer journey frame?
Did I translate features into outcomes — or did I present technically?
Did I ask excitement questions and let them speak, or did I keep presenting?
Did I recognise buying signals and close on them — or keep pitching?
What would I do differently in next week's demos?
🚀
Next Week Planning
Set intentions before the week starts
How many calls will I make next week? (Target: 150+)
Which follow-ups need to happen Monday morning?
Are there any demos already booked for next week to prepare for?
What's the one skill I will specifically focus on improving next week?
What will I do differently based on what didn't work this week?
🗓️ Monthly Review Framework
Once a month, zoom out. Look at the big picture. This is where strategic decisions get made — which niches to expand into, which parts of the system to double down on, and what your business looks like at scale.
💰
Revenue & Client Review
The business numbers
How many clients signed this month? How does that compare to last month?
What is my total monthly recurring revenue (MRR) from hosting retainers?
Did any clients churn (cancel)? If so, why?
Are existing clients happy? When did I last check in with each one?
Are there upsell opportunities with any existing clients (SEO, extra pages, etc)?
📈
Sales System Review
What's working, what isn't
What was my average call-to-demo rate this month? Is it improving?
What was my average demo-to-close rate? Is it improving?
Which objections came up most frequently? Do I handle them well?
Are there patterns in who converts vs who doesn't (city, dealership size, platform)?
What single change to my system would have the biggest impact next month?
🗺️
Strategy & Growth Review
Zoomed out — the big picture
Should I expand to a new city or niche, or deepen focus in the current one?
Are there referral systems I can activate from happy existing clients?
What does my business look like if I maintain this month's pace for 6 more months?
Is there anything I should stop doing that's taking time without producing results?
What would doubling my monthly closes require? More calls? Better demos? Both?
