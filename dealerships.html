📱 How Nigerian Dealerships Currently Get Customers
Every platform they use has a critical weakness. Learn these — they're the foundation of your pitch.
📸
Instagram
Their primary marketing channel. They post photos of available cars, prices in captions, and direct all interested buyers to WhatsApp DMs. Some run paid ads that link directly to their IG profile.
Posts disappear in feeds within hours. Old inventory posts are buried. No searchable catalogue. Customers have to scroll through weeks of posts to find a specific car. No pricing structure.
👥
Facebook
Used for Facebook Marketplace listings and Facebook Business Pages. Some post in large car-buying groups like "Cars for Sale in Lagos." Older demographic of buyers use Facebook more than Instagram.
Facebook Marketplace listings expire. Group posts get buried quickly. No brand consistency — each post looks different. Customers can't compare inventory in one place.
🎵
TikTok
A growing number of dealerships post car walkthrough videos on TikTok to reach younger buyers. Some have gotten viral videos with hundreds of thousands of views driving inquiries.
Viral reach is unpredictable — they can't rely on it. Even when a video goes viral, all interested buyers are still directed to WhatsApp DMs with no organised follow-up system.
💬
WhatsApp
The final destination for almost every lead. Customers DM to ask about prices, availability, and specs. Many dealerships use WhatsApp Business with a catalogue feature, but it's manual and limited.
Every single inquiry requires manual response. The same questions are answered 20 times a day. No way to show full inventory at once. Conversations get lost. Follow-ups fall through the cracks.
😤 Their Real Frustrations — The Pain You're Solving
These are not assumptions. These are the daily realities of running a Nigerian car dealership on social media alone. When you speak these out loud on a call, they'll feel heard.
🔁
Answering the same questions every single day
"Oga, what cars do you have?" "Do you have Camry?" "What is the price of the Highlander?" "Is the 2019 Venza still available?" — these messages come in all day, every day, on WhatsApp, Instagram DMs, and Facebook.
The sales team spends hours answering these manually. It's exhausting and pulls them away from actually closing deals. A website with a searchable inventory solves this completely.
🕳️
Old posts disappearing — inventory is invisible
They posted a 2020 Toyota Corolla three weeks ago. It's still available. But the post is buried under 40 newer posts and nobody can find it. They have to keep reposting the same cars or customers never see them.
A website keeps every car permanently visible and searchable. Customers find exactly what they're looking for without the dealership doing anything extra.
🤷
Customers can't compare vehicles on their own
A customer is deciding between a 2018 Camry and a 2019 Accord. On Instagram, they have to scroll through random posts trying to find both, often giving up and going somewhere else. The dealership loses the sale and never knows why.
A website lets customers filter by make, model, year, and price — and compare side by side. Buyers who can compare easily convert at much higher rates.
💸
Paying for ads that lead to WhatsApp chaos
They spend ₦50,000–₦200,000 on Instagram or Facebook ads per month. The ads drive traffic to their profile or WhatsApp. Hundreds of people inquire. The team can't keep up. Leads go cold. The ad spend is wasted.
A website turns ad traffic into structured leads — contact forms, enquiry submissions, and car-specific interest forms that the team can work through systematically. Every naira spent on ads works harder.
🌙
Missing customers who search at night
At 11pm, someone in Abuja is searching Google for "used Toyota Camry Lagos." The dealership has a perfectly matching car. But there's no website. The customer finds a competitor and buys from them the next day.
A website works 24/7. It captures leads even when the team is asleep. A well-structured contact form or WhatsApp link on a website converts late-night browsers into morning enquiries.

📞 Using This Knowledge on the Call
How to translate everything you've learned into specific language that resonates with dealership owners.
Opening — mention something specific "I was on your Instagram page this morning and I saw the 2020 Highlander you posted last week — really nice unit. But I noticed someone in the comments was asking for the price and nobody had replied yet. I think you're losing customers right there."
Pain identification "Can I ask — how many WhatsApp messages do you get daily asking about which cars you have available? Because I'm guessing that's taking up a lot of your team's time."
Creating curiosity "I actually built something for your dealership already — a full website where all your cars are listed, people can filter by make and model, and anyone interested clicks a button that sends you their details directly. I just want to show it to you for 15 minutes."
If they say "we use WhatsApp fine" "I understand — and WhatsApp is great for conversation. But what happens to the customer who's searching Google at 11pm for a used Corolla in Lagos? Right now, your dealership doesn't exist for that person. A website changes that."
