Demo Presentation

The demo call is where deals are won or lost. You have 15 minutes. Every minute matters. This module teaches you how to present the website as a business outcome — not a technical product — and how to naturally walk from the demo into a closed deal.

🧠 The Demo Mindset — Outcomes, Not Features
Most people show a website like they're showing off code. You will show it like you're revealing a business solution. The difference determines whether they sign or shrug.
Features vs Outcomes — always translate
"This is the homepage with a hero banner and navigation menu."
"This is the first thing a customer sees — it immediately tells them you're a serious, professional dealership before they've even looked at a single car."
"Here's the inventory page with filter options."
"This is where customers stop sending you 'what cars do you have' messages — they browse, filter by make and model, and find exactly what they want on their own."
"Each car has a detail page with specs and photos."
"When a customer is interested in a car, they see every photo and spec without having to ask — so by the time they message you, they're already decided. You're closing a warm lead, not starting from scratch."
"There's a contact form and enquiry button."
"Instead of juggling 50 WhatsApp messages from random people, you get structured enquiries from people who are already serious — name, number, exactly which car they want. Your team works through a clean list, not a chaotic chat."
"The website is mobile-responsive."
"85% of your customers will view this on their phone. It looks perfect on every device — same experience whether they're on a Samsung or an iPhone."
The demo mantra — say this to yourself before every call "I am not showing them a website. I am showing them what their business looks like with a proper online presence. Every section I click through is a problem they currently have — solved."
✅ Pre-Demo Checklist
Complete every item before the demo call starts. A prepared demo is a confident demo.
Website is live, tested, and all links work
Website has the dealership's actual name and branding
Sample inventory is populated with real car types they sell
Tested on mobile — looks clean on a phone
Enquiry form works and sends a test submission
WhatsApp CTA button works and links to correct number
Rehearsed the walkthrough at least twice out loud
Have the website URL ready to share instantly
Researched the dealership again — know 2-3 current things about their inventory
Pricing / retainer numbers are clear in my head
Internet connection is stable
I'm in a quiet place with no interruptions
⏱️ The 15-Minute Demo Walkthrough
This is the exact structure of every demo call. Time each phase. Don't rush — but don't let any phase run over.
1
Open Strong — Set the Frame 0–2 min
Before sharing the screen or link, set the context. Tell them what they're about to see and how to experience it.
Opening frame "Before I share the link, I want to give you some context. What I built is not a template — it's designed specifically for [Dealership Name]. I want you to experience it like one of your customers would. So when I share the link, I'd love you to open it on your phone first — because that's how most of your buyers will see it."
Have you ever tried to browse for a specific car on Instagram and couldn't find it easily? That's exactly the problem we solved here.
2
Share the Link — Let it Load 2–3 min
Send the website URL on WhatsApp. Let them open it. Stay quiet for 20–30 seconds while they take their first look.
While they're loading "I'll send it to you on WhatsApp right now… [sends link] Take your time, have a look at the first thing you see. Don't click anything yet — just take in the first impression."
⚡ The silence rule: After they open it, don't talk for at least 15 seconds. Let them react naturally. Their first reaction — "oh, this looks good" or "wow" — is your most powerful moment.
3
Walk the Customer Journey 3–9 min
Walk them through each section as if they are the customer. Frame everything around what the customer experiences — not what you built.
Section by section narration:
Homepage
First impression = instant credibility
"This is what a customer sees the moment they land. Before they've looked at a single car, they already know [Dealership Name] is a serious, professional operation. That credibility is built in 3 seconds — before you say a word."
Inventory / Cars Page
No more "what cars do you have?"
"This is where everything changes. Every car you have — right here, searchable. A customer looking for a Toyota SUV under 2020 just filters here and finds it immediately. No WhatsApp message needed. No waiting for your team to reply."
Individual Car Page
The DM request is already answered
"When they tap any car, they see every photo, full specs, price — everything they'd normally DM to ask. So by the time they reach out, they already want that specific car. Your team isn't explaining, they're closing."
Enquiry / Contact
Structured leads, not chaos
"When they're ready, they click here. Name, number, which car they want. That comes straight to you — organised, no back-and-forth. Your sales team works through a clean list every morning instead of digging through WhatsApp chats."
How many messages does your team answer every day about which cars you have? Imagine if that number dropped to almost zero.
If a customer found this on Google at midnight and sent an enquiry, what would your morning look like tomorrow?
4
Build Excitement — Ask, Don't Tell 9–12 min
Stop presenting. Start asking. Let them articulate the value in their own words.
The excitement questions "What do you think so far?"

"Which part would make the biggest difference for your team day-to-day?"

"If your customers could use this starting tomorrow, what do you think would change for them?"

"Honestly — does this look like something that fits how your dealership works?"
🧠 Why asking works: When they answer these questions out loud, they are selling themselves. "The inventory page would really help" coming from their mouth is worth 10× more than you saying it. Your job in this phase is to listen and affirm — not pitch.
5
Transition to Close 12–15 min
Once excitement is established, move naturally toward the next step. See Module 8 for full closing scripts.
Natural transition "I'm glad it resonates. So here's what happens next — the website is yours to keep, completely built. What we'd put in place is a small monthly arrangement to keep it hosted, maintained, and updated as your inventory changes. It's designed to be very manageable for a dealership your size. Should I walk you through how that works?"

💬 Handling Reactions During the Demo
What to say when they react — positively or with pushback.
✓ Positive reactions
"This looks good / I like it"
"I'm glad it resonates. Which part stands out most to you?"
"How much does it cost?"
"Great — let me finish the walkthrough and then I'll walk you through exactly how it works. Just a couple more minutes." [This is a buying signal — don't answer yet, finish the demo first]
"Can you add [specific feature]?"
"Yes — that's exactly the kind of thing we can build into it. Note it down and we'll include it. What else would make this perfect for your dealership?"
"Wow, you put our name on it"
"Of course — this was built specifically for [Dealership Name]. Every part of it is designed for how your customers shop for cars."
⚠ Hesitation during demo
"I need to show my partner"
"Of course — the link is already on your WhatsApp, just forward it. Should we schedule a quick 10-minute call with both of you so I can answer any questions directly?"
"It's okay but I'm not sure"
"That's fair. What specifically would make it a clearer yes for you? Is it a feature, the cost, or something else? I want to address whatever's holding you back."
"We'll think about it"
"No problem. Can I ask — what would you need to think through? I might be able to answer it right now and save you the time."
"It's not loading well"
"Let me share my screen instead — or I can walk you through it verbally while you try on a different connection. Bear with me." [Stay calm, have a backup plan]
🎯 Transitioning to the Close
The demo ends and the close begins here. The transition should feel natural — not like a gear change.
The 3-step transition bridge
1
Confirm they like it
"So overall — does this feel like something that would genuinely help [Dealership Name]?"
2
Introduce what happens next
"Great. So here's how it works — the website build is yours, completely done. What we put in place is a simple monthly arrangement to keep it hosted, maintained, and updated. It's very manageable — and it pays for itself the moment you get one extra enquiry from it."
3
Ask for the commitment
"Shall we get this live for you? I can have it fully set up within 48 hours — domain, hosting, everything running. You just say the word."
