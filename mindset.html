Sales Mindset

Before you dial a single number, your mindset is either your biggest asset or your biggest liability. This module rewires how you think about sales calls so you show up with confidence every single time.

💰 Why Business Owners Buy
Understanding their motivation is the foundation of every successful sales call.
😰
They buy to escape pain
The car dealership owner is tired of answering the same WhatsApp messages every day. "What cars do you have?" "What's the price of the Highlander?" A website removes that pain. You're selling relief, not a website.
📈
They buy to gain advantage
If their competitor down the road has a website and they don't, customers who Google "car dealers in Lagos" will never find them. You're offering a competitive edge. That's powerful.
🤝
They buy from people they trust
Nigerians especially buy on relationships. If you come across as a genuine person who did their research and truly wants to help, the sale becomes natural. Trust first, pitch second.
Key insight to internalize You are not selling a product. You are solving a problem. The moment you fully believe that — your calls will sound completely different. Desperation comes from needing the sale. Confidence comes from knowing you have the solution to their problem.
🚫 Why People Reject Sales Calls
Rejection is almost never personal. Here's what's really happening when someone says no.
⚡
They're caught off guard
You interrupted their day. They were in the middle of something. The rejection is not about you — it's about timing. Your job is to quickly earn the right to 30 more seconds.
🛡️
They have their guard up
Every Nigerian business owner has been called by people trying to sell them things they don't need. They've built a wall. Your job is not to break the wall — it's to make them lower it voluntarily by showing genuine curiosity.
❓
They don't see the value yet
They don't understand what a website would actually do for their business. This is not their failure — it's your opportunity to educate them in a way that clicks.
🕐
The timing is wrong
Sometimes they're genuinely busy. A polite follow-up call tomorrow often reaches a completely different person. Persistence beats talent in cold calling.
✗ Beginner thinking
They rejected me personally
I must have said the wrong thing
This doesn't work in Nigeria
They already have everything they need
I should give up after 2 nos
✓ StoreLift thinking
They rejected the interruption, not me
They just didn't see value yet — next call
Nigerian SMEs are wide open and underserved
They have a pain they haven't solved yet
The 10th call often converts what the 1st couldn't
🧘 How to Remove Desperation
Desperation is the single biggest killer of sales calls. People can hear it in your voice within 3 seconds.
📊
Think in batches, not individual calls
If you need each call to succeed, you'll sound desperate on every single one. Instead, think: "I'm working through 30 calls today. Some will say yes, most won't — and that's exactly how this works." That mindset shift is everything.
🎯
Your goal is the next call, not the close
On a cold call, you are not trying to close a client. You are trying to book a 15-minute demo call. That's it. When your goal is small, you sound relaxed. When you sound relaxed, people trust you.
The abundance mindset — say this to yourself before calling "There are thousands of car dealerships in Nigeria advertising on Instagram with no website. My only job is to find the ones who are ready to talk today. Every no eliminates one and brings me closer to a yes."
What detachment sounds like on a call Instead of: "Please sir, just give me a chance, I promise it will be worth it..."

Say: "Look, I built something for your dealership — if you like it when I show you, great. If not, no problem at all. I just wanted to put it on the table."
💪 How to Sound Confident
Confidence is not something you feel first. It's something you perform first — until you feel it.
🗣️
Slow down your speech
Nervous people speak fast. Confident people speak at a deliberate pace. Force yourself to slow down by 20% on every call, especially your opener. Pausing is power.
📢
Project your voice
Speak from your chest, not your throat. Stand up while calling — it opens your diaphragm and your voice automatically carries more authority. Nigerian business owners respond to energy.
😁
Smile — literally
Put a slight smile on your face before the call connects. It changes the warmth of your voice in a way the other person feels even over the phone. Put a small mirror by your desk to check your expression.
💬
Use their name early
"Good morning, is this [Name]?" — using someone's name signals you did your research and you're not randomly dialling. It immediately makes the call more personal and harder to dismiss.
⚖️
Be willing to walk away
Nothing sounds more confident than "If it's not a good time, no problem at all — I can reach out another day." Giving them an exit makes them want to stay.
📋
Know your script cold
Confidence comes from preparation. If you're thinking about what to say next, you can't focus on listening. Practise your opener until it's automatic.
🔥 Entering Flow State Before Calling
Flow state is when everything feels effortless. You can engineer it with a pre-call routine.
😰
Anxious
Heart racing, rushing your words, afraid of rejection. Almost everyone starts here.
😐
Mechanical
Reading from the script, sounding robotic. Common in the first week.
😌
Comfortable
You know the script, you're relaxed. This is where most people plateau.
🔥
Flow State
Fully present, listening deeply, responding naturally. This is the target.

1
The 2-Minute Power Warm-Up
⏱ 2 minutes · Do before your first call of the day
⌄
1Stand up straight. Shoulders back. Chin up.
2Take 3 slow, deep breaths — in for 4 counts, hold for 4, out for 6.
3Say your opener out loud — not in your head. Hear your own voice say the words confidently.
4Smile. Put that smile on before the call connects and keep it there.
5Dial.
2
The Mirror Drill
⏱ 5 minutes · Do once a week
⌄
1Stand in front of a mirror.
2Deliver your full cold call script — opener, offer, close — out loud.
3Watch your facial expressions. Are you frowning? Tensing up?
4Practise maintaining a relaxed, confident expression even when you say the hard parts.
5Do this until the script feels as natural as a normal conversation.
3
The Rejection Reset
⏱ 30 seconds · Do after every difficult call
⌄
1After a rude rejection or a tough call, pause. Don't immediately dial again.
2Say out loud: "That was one call. It doesn't define the next one."
3Take one deep breath.
4Make a note of what happened — not to dwell on it, but to learn from it.
5Dial the next number with a clean slate.
4
The Call Recording Review
⏱ 10 minutes · Do at end of each day
⌄
1Record your calls using your phone's built-in recorder or a call recorder app.
2Pick 3 calls from today — one good, one average, one where you felt you lost them.
3Listen back. Notice: did you rush? Did you sound apologetic? Did you get interrupted and lose your train of thought?
4Write down one specific thing to improve tomorrow.
5This single habit separates good salespeople from great ones.
🌅 Daily Mental Preparation Routine
What you do before you start calling determines the quality of every call.
Morning Routine
6:00 – 6:30am
Read your why
Why are you building StoreLift? What does closing your first client mean? Write it down and read it every morning.
6:30 – 7:00am
Research 5–10 dealerships on Instagram
Find their page. Note one specific thing about their inventory or posts to mention on the call.
7:00 – 7:10am
Read your script out loud
Not in your head. Out loud. Every single morning until it's second nature.
7:10am
Start calling
Business owners in Nigeria are active from 7am. Early calls catch them before the day gets hectic.
Common Beginner Mistakes
Mistake #1
Explaining too much on the first call
Your only goal is to book the demo. Stop at "I want to show you something I built for your dealership." Don't explain hosting, pricing, or tech.
Mistake #2
Asking open-ended questions at the close
Never ask "when are you free?" Ask "Is tomorrow morning or afternoon better for you?" Binary choices get answers.
Mistake #3
Giving up after the first objection
The first objection is almost always automatic. Stay calm, acknowledge it, then pivot with a question. Most sales happen after 3–4 objections.
Mistake #4
Not sending the WhatsApp immediately
Within 2 minutes of booking a demo, send the WhatsApp. Nigerian business owners are busy — they'll forget. Your message is the anchor.
Mistake #5
Sounding like you're reading a script
The script is training wheels. Learn it cold, then put it away. The goal is natural conversation — not a monologue.
