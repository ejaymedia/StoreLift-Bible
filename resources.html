⚡ Quick Reference Cards
📞 Cold Call Structure
1. Confirm name · smile · slow
2. Specific observation about their page
3. Name the gap / pain
4. "I built something for your dealership"
5. Free · 15 minutes · no commitment
6. "Mornings or afternoons?" — binary close
7. Get WhatsApp · send immediately
🖥️ Demo Structure (15 min)
0–2m Set frame · open on phone
2–3m Share link · stay silent 15 sec
3–9m Walk customer journey
9–12m Ask excitement questions
12–15m Transition to close
Translate features → outcomes always
💰 Pricing Sequence
1. "Build is completely free"
2. Explain what monthly covers
3. State price — pause — say nothing
4. "One car sale pays for months"
5. "Month-to-month, no contract"
Never apologise for the price
🚫 Never Say These
✗ "Sorry to bother you..."
✗ "I just wanted to quickly..."
✗ "When are you free?"
✗ "Please just give me a chance"
✗ "It's not expensive at all"
✗ "We are the best in the market"
✗ "How much for a website?"
🛡️ Objection Framework
1. Acknowledge — don't argue
2. Ask a clarifying question
3. Reframe toward the real pain
4. Pivot back to demo ask
First objection = almost always automatic
Most deals close after 3–4 objections
📡 Buying Signals
→ "How much does it cost?"
→ "Can you add our logo?"
→ "When can it be live?"
→ "I want to show my team"
→ "This is exactly what we need"
Signal = stop pitching, start closing

📋 Pre-Call Checklist
Run through this before every calling session. It takes 5 minutes and doubles your call quality.
🧠 Mindset
0 / 5
I am solving a problem — not selling a product. My mindset is service, not desperation.
Rejection is information, not personal failure. Every no brings me closer to a yes.
My only goal is to book the 15-minute demo. Nothing more, nothing less.
I am thinking in batches of 30 calls — not as individual calls that must each succeed.
I am willing to walk away from any call that isn't working. Detachment = confidence.
📋 Preparation
0 / 6
I have researched at least 10 dealerships on Instagram with specific observations noted for each.
I know a specific car model, post, or unanswered comment for at least 5 dealerships on my list.
I have read my opening script out loud at least twice this morning.
I have reviewed the objections I struggled with most yesterday.
My call list is ready — names, numbers, dealerships, observations.
I have checked for any follow-ups due today from previous conversations.
🎙️ Physical & Environment
0 / 5
I am standing or sitting upright — not slouched. My posture affects my voice.
I have drunk water and warmed up my voice — said a few sentences out loud before the first call.
I am in a quiet place where I won't be interrupted during calls.
My phone is charged and my network connection is stable.
I have a smile on my face right now — I can feel it — and I will keep it there on every call.

🚫 The 25 Most Common Mistakes
These are the specific habits that kill deals. Read through and honestly identify which ones you're guilty of.
On the Cold Call
✗ Starting with "Sorry to bother you"
Start with "Hello, good day — please is this [Name]?" You have nothing to apologise for.
✗ Speaking too fast when nervous
Slow down by 20% deliberately. Pause after their name. Pause after the pain point. Silence is power.
✗ Explaining too much on the first call
Your only job is to book the demo. Stop at the offer — don't explain hosting, pricing, or tech.
✗ Asking "when are you free?"
Always binary: "mornings or afternoons?" Open questions get "I'll let you know" which means never.
✗ Generic opening with no research
Mention a specific car, post, or unanswered comment. Generic = forgotten instantly. Specific = credibility.
✗ Giving up after the first objection
First objection is almost always automatic. Stay calm, acknowledge, and pivot with a question.
✗ Not sending WhatsApp immediately
Within 2 minutes of every booked call. Not 30 minutes. Not "later." Immediately. That's the rule.
✗ Sounding like you're reading a script
The script is training wheels. Learn it cold, then have a real conversation. Listen and respond.
On the Demo & Close
✗ Presenting features instead of outcomes
"Inventory page with filters" → "Customers find the exact car they want without messaging you." Always translate.
✗ Talking during the silence after sharing the link
Share the link. Stop talking for 15 seconds minimum. Let their first reaction happen naturally.
✗ Giving the price before showing the demo
Price before value = objection guaranteed. Value first, then price, then ROI framing, then remove risk.
✗ Apologising for the price
State the price confidently. Pause. Say nothing. "It's only ₦X" signals you think it's expensive.
✗ Discounting before they've objected
Never offer a discount unprompted. You're giving money away. Let them push back first.
✗ Pitching after a buying signal
"How much does it cost?" = stop pitching, start closing. Missing this is one of the costliest mistakes.
✗ Accepting "I'll pay later" without a date
Get a specific payment date confirmed while on the call. "Later" without a date is almost always never.
✗ Disappearing after payment
Over-communicate during setup. Every step confirmed on WhatsApp. Trust = repeat business + referrals.
Mindset & System Mistakes
✗ Treating each call as pass/fail
Think in batches. 30 calls today. Some yes, most no — that's the math. Volume + consistency = results.
✗ Skipping the end-of-day review
30 minutes of daily review compounds your improvement faster than anything else. Never skip it.
✗ Not recording and listening to calls
You can't fix what you can't hear. Record every call. Listen back weekly. One insight per session is enough.
✗ Building websites before booking demos
Build after the call, not before. Spend your time on calls first — demos come from calls, not from perfect sites.
✗ Not asking for referrals from happy clients
Two weeks after go-live, ask directly. Happy clients are your cheapest, highest-converting lead source.
✗ Losing momentum after a bad day
Bad days are part of the system. Reset with the rejection reset exercise and start fresh the next morning.
📖 Sales Terminology Glossary
Every term used in the Sales Bible defined in plain English.
Cold Call
A sales call made to a prospect who has had no prior contact with you. Called "cold" because there's no existing relationship to warm it up.
Warm Lead
A prospect who has already shown some interest — replied to your WhatsApp, watched the demo, or was referred by a client. Much easier to close than a cold lead.
Gatekeeper
The person who answers the phone and controls access to the decision-maker. Could be a receptionist, sales rep, or assistant.
Decision Maker
The person with authority to say yes or no to a purchase. In Nigerian dealerships, this is almost always the owner or a senior partner.
Pain Point
A specific problem or frustration the prospect experiences regularly. The most powerful pain points are ones they're already aware of — you just name them out loud.
Open Loop
An unanswered question or incomplete statement that creates psychological tension. "I built something for your dealership" is an open loop — the brain needs to close it by finding out what it is.
Buying Signal
A verbal or behavioural cue that indicates a prospect has internally decided to buy. "How much does it cost?" "When can it be live?" "Can you add our logo?" — all buying signals.
Assumptive Close
A closing technique where you proceed as if the prospect has already decided to buy. "Let me get the setup started — what domain name would you like?" assumes they said yes.
Objection
A stated reason why a prospect won't move forward. Almost always a question in disguise — "we don't have budget" = "I don't see enough value yet."
Anchor
The first number mentioned in a price negotiation, which becomes the reference point for all comparisons. "The build is free" (₦0) anchors before the monthly fee is mentioned.
Loss Aversion
The psychological principle that losing something hurts twice as much as gaining the same thing feels good. "You're losing customers every night at 11pm" is more motivating than "you could gain customers."
MRR
Monthly Recurring Revenue — the total predictable revenue you receive each month from active clients paying their hosting retainer.
Pipeline
The set of all active prospects at various stages: cold leads, contacted, demo booked, demo held, negotiating, closed. Managing the pipeline means knowing where everyone is and what the next action is.
Churn
When a paying client cancels their subscription. Reducing churn means keeping clients happy through consistent service and check-ins.
Conversion Rate
The percentage of prospects who move to the next stage. Call-to-demo conversion rate, demo-to-close conversion rate — tracking these tells you exactly where to improve.
Upsell
Selling an additional service to an existing client after the relationship is established. SEO, extra pages, social media management — these are StoreLift upsells.
Retainer
A recurring monthly fee. Your hosting and maintenance fee is a retainer — clients pay it monthly to keep the service active.
Social Proof
Evidence that others similar to the prospect have made the same decision successfully. Client testimonials, portfolio examples, and referrals are all forms of social proof.
ROI (Return on Investment)
The value returned relative to cost. "One extra car sale per month pays for the hosting 10× over" is an ROI argument — connecting the monthly fee to business outcomes the owner already understands.
