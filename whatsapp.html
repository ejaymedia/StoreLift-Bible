💡 WhatsApp Messaging Rules for Nigerian Clients
⏰
Send within 2 minutes of hanging up. Nigerian business owners have busy days. If you wait an hour, they've forgotten the conversation entirely. Send while you're still fresh in their mind.
📵
Never send between 10pm and 7am. Respect their personal time. Messages that arrive late at night feel intrusive and are often ignored or cause irritation.
📏
Keep messages short. Nigerian business owners skim long WhatsApp messages. Your key point should be visible in the preview without opening. If it needs scrolling on a phone screen, it's too long.
🙋
Always use their name. "Good morning [Name]" feels personal. "Good morning" feels like a broadcast. Personalisation increases open and response rates significantly.
✅
One clear action per message. Don't ask three questions in one message. One message, one ask. "Is morning or afternoon better?" not "When are you free? Also, should I add more cars? And what do you think of the colours?"
🔕
3-message rule for no response. If someone doesn't reply after 3 well-spaced follow-up messages, move them to the "Re-engage in 3 weeks" category. Persistence is good; harassment is not.
😊
Use emojis sparingly and strategically. One or two emojis make a message feel warmer and more human. A message full of emojis looks unprofessional. 👋 to open, 🙏 to close — that's usually enough.
🔗
Include the website link every time you mention the demo. Don't make them search for it. Every message that references the website should contain the link so they can tap and view immediately.
